The Toxic Truth
What's hiding inside your old device

Your phone is a cocktail of poisons. Disposed wrongly, it kills.

A single smartphone contains over 60 chemical elements — many of them deadly. When burned in the open or dissolved in acid baths by informal recyclers, these toxins are released into the air we breathe, the water we drink, and the soil that grows our food. This is not a distant problem. It is happening in Indian cities right now.

Lead — brain damage Mercury — nerve damage Cadmium — kidney failure Arsenic — cancer risk Beryllium — lung disease Chromium VI — DNA damage BFRs — hormonal disruption
Who Gets Harmed

Children

Lead and mercury accumulate in growing bodies, causing irreversible neurological and developmental damage.

Water Sources

Heavy metals leach into groundwater near dump sites, contaminating drinking water for entire communities.

Soil & Crops

Toxic residues poison farmland for decades. Cadmium absorbed by plants enters the food chain silently.

Air We Breathe

Open burning of circuit boards releases dioxins and furans — among the most dangerous airborne carcinogens known.
